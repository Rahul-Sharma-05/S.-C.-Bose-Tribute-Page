Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

"The Unsung Worrior of Indian Freedom Struggle"

Here's the Timeline of Subhas Chandra Bose:

  • 1897 - Born on 23 January in Cuttack (in state of Odisha in India).
  • 1909 - 12-year-old Subhas Bose followed his five brothers to the Ravenshaw Collegiate School in Cuttack.
  • 1912 - He secured the second position in the matriculation examination conducted under the auspices of the University of Calcutta.
  • 1919 - At his father's urging, Subhas Bose agreed to travel to England to prepare and appear for the Indian Civil Services (ICS) examination.
  • 1921 - Left ICS because he had to work under the British.
  • 1921 - Bose came back to India from London.
  • 1923 - Bose was elected the President of Indian Youth Congress and also the Secretary of Bengal State Congress.
  • 1925 - Bose was arrested and sent to prison in Mandalay, where he contracted tuberculosis.
  • 1927 - After being released from prison, Bose became general secretary of the Congress party and worked with Jawaharlal Nehru for independence.
  • 1928 - Bose organised the Annual Meeting of the Indian National Congress in Calcutta.
  • 1930 - Bose became mayor of Calcutta.
  • 1938 - Bose accepted nomination as the INC Indian National Congress presidential candidate
  • 1939 - Bose organised the All India Forward Bloc a faction within the Indian National Congress
  • 1941 - Bose escaped from India under British surveillance to Nazi Germany. Met Adolf Hitler and ask help for Indian Independence struggle.
  • 1943 - Went to Japan and led Indian National Army (Azaj Hind Fauj).
  • 1943 - Netaji addressed the army as "Supreme Commander" in front of the Town Hall of Singapore, and gave the slogan "Delhi Chalo". He also gave the slogan "Jai Hind" and "You give me blood, I will give you freedom".
  • 1945 - Subhas Chandra Bose's death occurred from third-degree burns on 18 August after his overloaded Japanese plane crashed in Japanese-ruled Formosa (now Taiwan).
  • Recent - The central government has decided to observe Subhas Chandra Bose Jayanti as Parakram Diwas to be celebrated on 23rd January.
